About Us

It was the mission of Late Umashankar Pal to bring the best of Kolkattaiya biryani at affordable prices - biryani by the Kolkatans; for the Kolkatans! That dream took flight when The Great Abar Khabo Restaurant (then named Uma Biryani House) got started from an unused makeshift garrage in Potoldanga back in 1982. Kolkatans cherished our biryani so much that Uma Biryani House was forced to shift to a new and spacious location. As of today, the TGAKR group boasts of eight outlets across Greater Kolkata and is in the process to set up shops at Bengaluru, Mumbai and Hyderabad.

Biryani Trivia

A Bit on the History of Biryani

While biryani is popularly associated with the Mughals, there is some historical evidence to show that there were other, similar rice dishes prior to the Mughal invasion. There is mention of a rice dish known as “Oon Soru” in Tamil as early as the year 2 A.D. Oon Soru was composed of rice, ghee, meat, turmeric, coriander, pepper, and bay leaf, and was used to feed military warriors.
